The Society for Adolescent Medicine concludes that corporal punishment in schools is an ineffective, dangerous, and unacceptable method of discipline.
Suspensions or expulsions shall be used only for serious instances of student misconduct.
Religion teaches that corporal punishment is acceptable and necessary.
A school system may waive or reduce the payment required if the student is from a family of low income and may provide for a method of payment other than lump-sum payment.
All parents of children summoned to Truancy Court will be charged court costs of $25.
